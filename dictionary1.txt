<tr valign="top" class="tr3"><td align="right" width="1%" class="td3n1">9.&nbsp;&nbsp;</td><td class="td3n2"><b>question of law</b> (in English law) that part of the issue before a court that is decided by the judge</td></tr>

<div class="dicTl">Word Origin & History</div>
<br/><span class="me">question</span><br /><div class="body">c.1300, from Anglo-Fr. questiun, O.Fr. question "legal inquest," from L. qu&aelig;stionem (nom. qu&aelig;stio) "a seeking, inquiry," from root of qu&aelig;rere (pp. qu&aelig;situs) "ask, seek" (see <a id="dic_result" href="/browse/query">query</a>). The verb is first recorded 1470, from O.Fr. questionner (13c.). Question </div><a class="more"></a><span class="moreun"><div class="body">mark is from 1869, earlier question stop (1862). Depreciatory sense of questionable is attested from 1806.</div><a class="less"></a></span>
